### About Me

Hi, I'm Md Fahim Rayhan.

I'm currently pursuing Master's degree in Image Analysis and Machine Learning at Uppsala University in Sweden. What I enjoy most about this field is the combination of mathematics, programming, and discovery—using data to understand complex systems and uncover patterns that aren't immediately visible.

My academic journey has taken me from data science and software development to increasingly research-oriented work, where I can explore challenging problems and learn from people across different disciplines. I am naturally curious, enjoy building things from scratch, and like turning ideas into practical solutions. Outside of academics, I enjoy working on side projects, exploring new technologies, and meeting people who are passionate about innovation and creating positive impact.



### Academic Background
My Master's studies at Uppsala University have exposed me to a broad range of topics across artificial intelligence, statistical learning, image analysis, and scientific computing. Through both coursework and projects, I have developed a strong interest in solving complex analytical problems and working with data-intensive systems.

In addition to my degree, I have completed courses in Graph Neural Networks, Reinforcement Learning, Big Data Analysis, and AI Ethics, which have helped me develop a broader understanding of modern AI from both technical and societal perspectives.

Previously, I completed a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Engineering at East West University, where I built my foundation in programming, data science, and software engineering.


### Projects
I enjoy working on projects that combine technology with real-world challenges.

Over the years, I have worked on machine learning applications, data-driven platforms, web development projects, and automation-focused solutions. One of my notable projects involved developing an agricultural analytics platform that leveraged data and predictive models to support decision-making in farming environments.

Beyond coursework, I often explore personal and experimental projects as a way to learn new technologies, test ideas, and improve my problem-solving skills.



### Interests

What motivates me most is learning and building.

I am particularly interested in emerging technologies, research-driven innovation, and interdisciplinary projects that bring together people from different backgrounds and expertise. I enjoy exploring new ideas, discussing research, and understanding how technology can be translated into practical solutions that create value for society.

I also have a strong interest in entrepreneurship and enjoy connecting technical knowledge with real-world applications.


### Current Tasks
At the moment, I am focusing on strengthening my research skills, expanding my understanding of advanced AI methods, and participating in projects that allow me to apply theory in practice.

I am also actively looking for opportunities to collaborate with researchers, students, and innovators who are working on challenging and impactful problems.

### Future Goals

Looking ahead, I hope to contribute to meaningful research while also building technologies that can be used beyond the laboratory and classroom.

My long-term ambition is to work at the intersection of research, innovation, and practical application, helping bridge the gap between academic discoveries and solutions that can make a difference in the real world.

I believe some of the most exciting advancements happen when people from different disciplines come together, and I hope to be part of that journey through collaboration, continuous learning, and curiosity.

  let currentIndex = 0;
  const slides = document.querySelectorAll('.slide');

  function showSlide(index) {
    if (index >= slides.length) {
      currentIndex = 0;
    } else if (index < 0) {
      currentIndex = slides.length - 1;
    } else {
      currentIndex = index;
    }
    const offset = -currentIndex * 100;
    document.querySelector('.slides').style.transform = `translateX(${offset}%)`;
  }

  function changeSlide(step) {
    clearInterval(autoSlideInterval);
    showSlide(currentIndex + step);
    autoSlideInterval = setInterval(() => changeSlide(1), 2000);
  }

  document.querySelector('.prev').addEventListener('click', () => changeSlide(-1));
